About me

Hi, I am Himanshu. I am an Electronics Engineer and Hobbyist Game Designer with over 12 years of game design and development experience. I have won several prestigious international game jams as a hobbyist, including grabbing 3rd place in Core Diversifier at Global Game Jam 2021. I hold several game design certifications from HP (Hewlett Packard)and I am continuously studying various game design literature to enhance my skills.

I have expertise in designing, developing, and publishing 2D, 3D, and XR (VR/AR) games. I’m skilled in using Godot, Unity Ads and AdMob, and I excel in research, analysis, game design documentation, and playtesting. I have experience as a Steam game modder and I am one of the earliest Roblox content creator since 2007. I also livestream regularly on Twitch, YouTube and Kick, playing retro games and game design streams. I am a passionate retro gamer and retro console and retro computer collector. I’ve started a young game studio in India and can work legally in India, the USA, and Canada without a visa or sponsorship. I’m excited to network, collaborate, and contribute to the gaming industry. I am also interested in internships.

Few Published Games

    • Turf Wars 2D Multiplayer: A 2D multiplayer PvP arena inspired by the single-player flash game RAZE by Addison Rodomista and Justin Goncalves.

    • Featuring Raze-like mechanics in a 2D platformer map, the game enables real-time multiplayer shooting combat using Photon PUN networking on the Unity4 engine.

    • Players can join existing rooms or create new ones, engaging in fast-paced, tactical shooter battles across multiple maps.

    • Published on Kongregate.com under a contract, the game draws its name from the multiplayer mechanics of GTA San Andreas Multi Theft Auto client's servers, where player gangs fought for control of Las Venturas territories/turf.

    • Check out the game on Kongregate: Turf Wars 2D Multiplayer (webgl required to play)

    • Flappy Rajinikanth: My first published game in 2013.

    • Inspired by the viral success of Flappy Bird that year, this 2D side-scroller pays tribute to South Indian superstar Rajinikanth, the Chuck Norris of India.

    • Developed through unity3d YouTube tutorials, the game humorously features Rajinikanth racing a jumbo jet in an auto rickshaw over the Mumbai skyline.

    • With simple yet addictive gameplay, it merges the iconic style of Rajinikanth with the charm of Flappy Bird.

    • The game was also published on Facebook.com using unity3d's webgl support at that time.
